Section 01

Brand Essence & Feel

The soul of the brand — what every design decision must serve.
“A quiet, living space for reflection, connection, and remembrance — where nothing is being sold, and nothing is being taught, only gently revealed.”

Core Principle

An invitation to reflection, not instruction — a calm and grounded space that gently guides visitors inward.

5 Guiding Adjectives

Calm · Warm · Spacious · Grounded · Intentional

The site should feel like quiet luxury meets editorial minimalism meets grounded spiritual presence.

Guiding Feeling

“Returning home to something already known.”

What This Is Not

  • Not a spiritual authority platform
  • Not promotional or conversion-driven
  • Not ornate, busy, or loud
  • Not instructional or dogmatic
  • Not a traditional website — it’s a living space

Type Glow Guidance

Typography may carry a soft glow treatment where appropriate — especially in threshold moments, reflections, companion lines, and other elevated display text. This glow should feel diffused, warm, and atmospheric rather than graphic or neon.

Use Glow Highlight, Soft Gold, and occasionally supporting colors in very restrained ways. Supporting colors may also be used for background design elements, transitions, layered surfaces, and select font-glow treatments, but never so heavily that calm, clarity, or legibility is reduced.

Accessibility — “Care as Cause”

Ivory on Espresso should be the primary high-contrast text treatment throughout the site. For CTA buttons, use Espresso text on Amber background for strongest clarity. Glow, accent, and supporting colors should enhance atmosphere, not reduce readability. Accessibility is non-negotiable.

Spacing Principles

  • Line height: 1.5–1.8 (generous, breathable)
  • Text width: 500–700px maximum (narrow, centered)
  • Letter spacing: Normal to slightly loose on headlines
  • Paragraph spacing: Generous — let text breathe

Avoid

  • Heavy bold usage (nothing above Medium/500)
  • Tight line spacing
  • Full-width text blocks
  • All caps (except sparingly for small UI labels)

Section 05

Layout & Spacing

Space is the primary design element. The page should feel like breathing.
  • Large margins on all sides
  • Generous vertical spacing between sections (80–120px+)
  • Centered content alignment
  • One idea per section — never stack competing messages
  • Text width: 500–700px maximum
  • Section backgrounds: primarily Espresso-led, with Soft Light, Warm Ivory, and supporting tones used selectively for rhythm, contrast, and transition

Page Rhythm

Expand, pause, expand, pause. Each section is a room in the house.

The darker base should feel grounding, not heavy, so contrast moments should be intentional and spacious rather than frequent.

Mobile

  • Mobile-first responsive design
  • Touch-friendly tap targets (44px minimum)
  • Even more generous spacing on mobile
  • Single-column layout
Section 06

Imagery & Visual Language

Images should feel like they were found, not produced.
Use
  • Light entering a space (windows, doorways, canopies)
  • Reflections (water, glass, mirrors)
  • Sky and horizon
  • Natural textures (linen, sand, stone, wood grain)
  • Warm hearth / firelight
  • Subtle geometric patterns (mandala-inspired)
  • Welcome Home mat — core symbolic motif
Avoid
  • Cliché spiritual imagery (lotus, third eyes, chakras)
  • Overly literal visuals
  • Busy compositions
  • Stock photos that feel staged or corporate
  • Heavy filters or dark moody aesthetics

Image Treatment

  • Soft, natural color grading (aligned with palette)
  • Warm tone overlay if needed
  • High quality, contemplative compositions
Section 07

Experience Direction

This is not a traditional website. It is a living space.

Homepage Flow

Section 0
Threshold — “Welcome Home”
A quiet, full-screen moment of arrival. Just “Welcome Home” in Cormorant on Warm Ivory. Subtle fade transition. Welcome Home mat integration. This transforms the site from content to experience.
Section 1
Rotating Reflection
A living element — quote, poem, or reflection that rotates daily/weekly. Cormorant italic. Amber accent.
Section 2
Hero
Brand name/logo. Headline: “A Quiet Place to Remember What Already Lives as Love.” CTA: Stay Connected. Companion Line: “Love is not separate from us; it is what we are, and all that is.”
Section 3
Intention
Centered paragraph. Minimal structure. Editorial tone.
Section 4
How It Unfolds
Narrow text. Increased spacing. Slows the experience.
Section 5
What’s Unfolding
“Rooms of the house” language: Pause & Remember (not Reflection) · Shared Listening (not Conversations) · Living Creativity (not Creative Expression)
Section 6
Invitation
Warm, open, human bridge to connection.
Section 7
Email Signup — “Threshold Deeper”
Espresso background. Ivory text. Amber CTA. Feels like stepping further into the house.
Section 8
Footer
Brand name. Grounding line. Optional closing reflection: “You are not meeting love here. You are remembering yourself as love.”

Motion

  • Slow fades (0.3–0.5s)
  • Gentle scroll-triggered reveals
  • Subtle hover states
  • No autoplay media
  • Motion should feel like breathing
